# PRODUCT.md — Invisible Scribe

> Locks strategic + voice decisions for invisiblescribe.com across sessions.
> Read this BEFORE writing any HTML. Created 2026-06-16 (EEC opt-in build).

## Register
`brand` — the design IS part of the product. Invisible Scribe is a thought-leadership
ghostwriting service; the opt-in page is the first proof that the founder behind it
can make plain things land. The page must read like it was made by someone who writes
for a living, not a template.

## What this is
A done-for-you thought-leadership writing service for B2B fintech founders. The
practitioner becomes the founder's voice (educational email courses + newsletters)
so the founder stays in the build while the market still hears from them.

The page being built: the opt-in for the free 5-day educational email course,
"The Fintech Founder's Authority Playbook" — the 5 mistakes that bury good
fintech founders, one fix each. The course IS the lead magnet and the proof.

## Who it's for
B2B fintech founders, roughly 1M to 15M ARR, who would rather build the product
than perform online, but know the market buys from whoever it trusts. The
"shadow expert": right, capable, and unheard.

## Three voice words
- Earned (every claim traces to lived work, never borrowed authority)
- Plain (clarity is the whole pitch; jargon is the enemy being sold against)
- Unhurried (editorial confidence, not landing-page urgency)

## Anti-references (do NOT look like these)
- Generic SaaS opt-in: centered headline + gradient + 3 feature cards + boxed form
- "Marketing guru" funnel page: countdown timers, fake scarcity, hype stacks
- The prior v1 of this page (dark + blue + Gloock, 2-col card grid, dead form),
  superseded; it predates the organic-geometry first-pass standard.

## Audience reflexes to reject
- Fintech founders are jargon-fluent and jargon-sick. No "rails / orchestration /
  seamless / secure / scalable." The page sells AGAINST that vocabulary.
- They distrust performers. No exclamation hype, no manufactured numbers, no
  testimonials (none exist yet, the practitioner story is the proof).
- They are short on time. One job on the page: opt in. One primary action.

## Success criterion
A fintech founder reads the hero, feels "this is me, right but unheard," and gives
first name + email to start the course. Composite copy battery >=90; design-audit
>=15/20, anti-patterns >=3/4.

## Failure mode
Reads as AI-generated within 5 seconds (rectangle tells, card grid, gradient bar),
OR sounds like a marketer guessing at a fintech founder's world, OR makes a claim
the founder can't back (invented client proof / numbers).

## Hard copy rules (from global battery)
- First person "I" / "Boubacar" only. Never third-person about himself.
- No em-dashes anywhere. No refund/guarantee language. No invented numbers.
- NEVER name internal codenames. Say "an autonomous operator I built to run my
  back office," never the system's internal name.
